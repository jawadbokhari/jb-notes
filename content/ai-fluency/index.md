# AI Fluency

> AI is not yet another tool to generate something — it's your thinking partner that you need to develop a relationship with.

Most of us start with AI the wrong way: we treat it like a search engine or a vending machine. Type something in, get something out. AI Fluency is the realization that this framing limits you.

AI Fluency isn't a technical skill. It's about developing an **intellectual partnership** — learning to collaborate with AI rather than just instruct it.

These are my notes from working through the [AI Fluency framework](https://anthropic.skilljar.com/ai-fluency-framework-foundations).

---

## The 4Ds

The framework organizes AI collaboration into four competencies:

- **[[Delegation]]** — Thoughtfully decide what to do yourself, what to collaborate on, and what to fully hand off
- **[[Description]]** — Communicate in ways that build shared understanding, not just issue commands
- **Discernment** — Evaluate AI outputs critically rather than accepting them at face value
- **Diligence** — Take responsibility for the work you do with AI

> [!tip] The key insight
> The goal isn't to automate everything. It's to create the most effective human-AI partnership for any given task.

---

## The Two Loops

The 4Ds aren't independent — they form two reinforcing loops.

### Delegation ↔ Diligence

You can't delegate well without knowing what you're responsible for. And you can't be diligent without understanding what you delegated in the first place.

| **Delegation** | **Diligence** |
|---|---|
| **Problem Awareness** — understand your goals and the work involved | **Creation Diligence** — be thoughtful about which AI systems you use and how |
| **Platform Awareness** — know what different AI systems can do | **Transparency Diligence** — be open about AI's role in your work |
| **Task Delegation** — divide work strategically between you and AI | **Deployment Diligence** — own the final outcome |

> Effective delegation requires both expertise in your field *and* an understanding of AI capabilities. Without the first, you can't judge the output. Without the second, you won't know what's worth delegating.

### Description ↔ Discernment

Every exchange with AI is a loop: you describe what you want, you evaluate what you get, and your evaluation informs how you describe better next time. The goal is to tighten this loop — narrowing the gap between what you meant and what AI produced.

---

## Why This Matters

Without AI Fluency, you either under-use AI (treating it as fancy autocomplete) or over-trust it (accepting outputs without judgment). Both are expensive mistakes.

Fluency is the middle path: you bring the expertise and judgment, AI brings the scale and speed. The partnership is only as strong as your ability to drive it.
