Delegation focuses on deciding what work to do yourself, what to collaborate on or delegate to  AI, and how to distribute tasks effectively.

> The goal isn't to automate everything, but to create the most effective human-AI partnership for any given task or goal.

## Problem Awareness
Do you know what's your problem? If not, please be clear about that first. 

> Delegation requires both expertise in your field and understanding of AI capabilities

If you do not have necessary expertise in the field you are trying to solve the problem with AI, you should first build expertise. AI can help you for that as well but your goalpost now changes from solving a problem in that domain to developing domain expertise. 

> BMAD brainstorming agents are really good in helping you sharpen your understanding using multiple brainstorming approaches


Assuming you have a good understanding of your problem, you should work with AI to develop a shared understanding of the problem. Let's call it a shared vision of the problem. 

> Use skills such as /bmad-analyst and other brainstorming agents to have a shared understanding of your problem. 


## Task Delegation
You got to know that what are the different parts of the work and what workflow would you adopt to solve it. 
For each part of the work: 
- Can it be solved with some deterministic program? 
- Should you use some machine learning algorithm or similar? or
- If it's some non-deterministic part of the problem, which AI system should this be delegated to?

> E.g. "Hi Claude, I'm preparing how to [insert task] and want to discuss with you what a delegation plan may look like for figuring out which parts I should delegate to an AI like you vs. not. Can you help me with this?"
