# AI Impact
The winners this year aren't the smallest companies; they are the ones using AI to make their people 10x more effective.

The more I learn AI, the more I feel the value of a human. We being human had lost ourselves in the roles we had been performing. With the Doer mindset we kept on doing a lot, following instructions more than asking questions. We kept hiding our questions under the fear of customers, our bosses, and at times our ego (what if this was a stupid question). We are tuned to do more with the least discovery of the problem or the situation under hand. 
AI is liberating me from all these fears. No boss, no customer, no ego. Keep on asking whatever you feel like. It'll be your discovery partner and go with you to the unexplored territories I never tried touching before. 
But, this journey is not any easy journey for me. I'll have to discover myself more with the ability to ask more questions. I have to hold my horses not to jump to conclusions faster and have to open up other angles to explore the problem. 

A problem is actually not a problem but a journey. It started with something and it grew to this point and it will take a certain direction because of the behavior of those surrounding it or treating it. Usually, you don't have to just fix the problem but also the people and their behavior as well.
